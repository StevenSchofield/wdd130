About Me

My name is Steven, and I am a hobby game designer. I am currently in college for my Computer Science degree. Born and raised in Idaho, and have had a love for programming and games since I was young.

Purpose

There are two activities that I want visitors to be able to complete on my site. First, I want people to be able to play my games, of course! But more importantly, I want people to be able to see how I think and how I work regarding game development. Thus, much of the development of this website will be on presenting the information relating to the development process. All the information needed to fill this site and complete any actions that this site offers is in my head or scattered across my notes. I just hope I can remember enough to fill it out. If need be, I can just develop another game and blog about my progress.

Why not just use itch.io?

Currently, my games are also being hosted on Itch.io, so it is understandable that one may be confused as to why I am making a whole other website to host my games. Iâ€™ll be honest, part of it is because I just wanted to make a website, and this seemed as good of an excuse as any. But since I want this to be a portfolio of my projects, and a demonstration of how I go about developing games, worlds, and stories, I want a blog format as well for future potential employers to browse and read. Plus, the fact that I was able to create a website to host all of this should be another point in my favor. If I was just to send them to itch, they would only be able to see my completed games, and all the information required to pick up and play. The entire design process and background information on the world and story is lost or difficult to find. If I have full control over the entire website, I can include all information in easy to access locations and can emphasize the information that is relevant to their study.
