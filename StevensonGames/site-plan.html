Overview

Purpose

While I am generally quite open to share my love of programming, what I often neglect to mention is how this love started. In the spirit of full honesty, as I child I wanted an excuse to play more video games. From this love of gaming grew a love of programming, which primarily has developed itself as a love of game design and development. I have made many games, and some of them I am actually fairly proud of. The issue I have come across is they are scattered. However, since they are all web-based, I can embed them all in a website. That is my goal for this assignment: to create a website to hold my creations as a sort of portfolio.

Audience

The audiance of this website will be friends, family, and potential employers.

Content

Home page

The purpose of this website is to provide a portfolio of my games and game ideas. Each game page will have a blog-like section that details my design process and the development of the mechanics and story of the games. Of course, there will also be links to play the games. This should be simple, as they all currently web based.

Games

This page will have links that leads to the two games I initially plan to display on here. One will be playable, the other only a concept.
Playable Game
Bubbles! Will be playable, as it is a finished game. I am particularly proud of it as I was able to make everything for it in the space of one week. I am also thinking about expanding it into a full game. Purpose
There are two activities that I want visitors to be able to complete on my site. First, I want people to be able to play my games, of course! But more importantly, I want people to be able to see how I think and how I work regarding game development. Thus, much of the development of this website will be on presenting the information relating to the development process. All the information needed to fill this site and complete any actions that this site offers is in my head or scattered across my notes. I just hope I can remember enough to fill it out. If need be, I can just develop another game and blog about my progress.

Why not just use itch.io? Currently, my games are also being hosted on Itch.io, so it is understandable that one may be confused as to why I am making a whole other website to host my games. Iâ€™ll be honest, part of it is because I just wanted to make a website, and this seemed as good of an excuse as any. But since I want this to be a portfolio of my projects, and a demonstration of how I go about developing games, worlds, and stories, I want a blog format as well for future potential employers to browse and read. Plus, the fact that I was able to create a website to host all of this should be another point in my favor. If I was just to send them to itch, they would only be able to see my completed games, and all the information required to pick up and play. The entire design process and background information on the world and story is lost or difficult to find. If I have full control over the entire website, I can include all information in easy to access locations and can emphasize the information that is relevant to their study.
